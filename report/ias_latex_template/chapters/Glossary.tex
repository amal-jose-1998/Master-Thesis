\addchap{Glossary}

\begin{description}
    \item [\ac{AV}] A vehicle that can drive by itself without human control. It uses sensors and artificial intelligence to understand the surrounding environment and make driving decisions.
    \item [\ac{HDV}] A vehicle that is controlled by a human driver who performs all driving tasks such as steering, accelerating, and braking.
    \item [\ac{DBN}] A probabilistic graphical model that uses a directed acyclic graph to represent time-series data, where each node represents a random variable and the edges show how these variables depend on each other within and across time steps.
    \item [\ac{DAG}] A graph made up of nodes and directed edges between them, where the edges do not form any closed loops.
    \item [Mixed Traffic] A traffic situation where both autonomous vehicles and human-driven vehicles operate together.
    \item [Trajectory] The time-ordered sequence describing the movement of a vehicle over time.
    \item [\ac{THW}] The time needed for a vehicle to reach the current position of the vehicle ahead if it maintains its current velocity.
    \item [\ac{TTC}] The time needed for a vehicle to collide with the vehicle ahead if both of them maintain their current velocity.
    \item [Latent State] A variable that is not directly observed but is inferred from data.
    \item [Emission Model] The probability distribution that describes how observations are generated from latent states.
    \item [Observation] The data that is directly measured from the system, represented as feature vectors. 
    \item [State Transition] Probability of transitioning from one latent state to another.
    \item [Inference] The process of determining the most likely latent states from the observed data.
    \item [Belief State] The probability distribution over the latent states given the observed data at a specific time step.
    \item [Likelihood] The probability of the observed data under a given model.
    \item [Prior] The probability distribution over the latent states before observing any data.
    \item [Posterior] The probability distribution over the latent states after observing the data.
    \item [\ac{MAP}] The estimate of the most likely latent state, given the observed data. It is the state that maximizes the posterior distribution.
    \item [Confusion Matrix] A matrix showing the performance of a classification model, across the true and predicted classes.
    \item [Precision] The ratio of true positive predictions to the total predicted positives. It indicates the accuracy of positive predictions.
    \item [Recall] The ratio of true positive predictions to the total actual positives. It indicates the completeness of positive predictions.
    \item [F1 Score] The harmonic mean of precision and recall.
    \item [Entropy] A measure of uncertainty in a probability distribution. It also quantifies the amount of information needed to describe the distribution.
\end{description}
